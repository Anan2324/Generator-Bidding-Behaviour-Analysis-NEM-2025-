⚡ Generator Bidding Behaviour Analysis (NEM 2025)

Project Objective: This portfolio explores bidding strategies in Australia’s National Electricity Market (NEM) using April 2025 generator data. The goal is to uncover behavioural patterns of fossil-fuel and renewable generators and derive business-relevant insights for market operators and policymakers.

📌 Problem Overview

With increasing penetration of renewables and volatile energy demand, understanding bidding behaviour is essential to ensure grid reliability and efficient pricing. This project identifies how generation technology influences pricing strategy and bid availability patterns.

🛠️ Methodology & Tools

  • Data Source: Public dispatch bidding data from the AEMO (April 2025)
  • Tech Stack: Python (pandas, matplotlib, seaborn), Jupyter Notebook, HTML/CSS
  • Approach: Data wrangling → Feature Engineering → Aggregation → Visual Analytics

🔍 Key Insights

  • ⚡ Renewables bid nearly all capacity at or below $0 — indicating price inelastic behaviour.
  • 🔥 Fossil-fuels bid across wide price ranges — showing responsive pricing strategy.
  • 🌅 Evening peaks are dominated by fossil fuels as solar bids drop after 6PM.
